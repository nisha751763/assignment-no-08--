inforamtion

Sunflowers are tall plants with large flower heads that get their name from following the sun. The large "flower" is actually a composite flower head called a capitulum, made of many smaller florets. The plant is scientifically known as Helianthus annuus and is valuable for its seeds, from which edible oil is extracted, and for its ornamental use.

Description and characteristics

Sunflowers can grow very tall, with some varieties reaching over 3 meters (10 feet). The stem is typically thick and hairy. Flower head: The large head, or capitulum, consists of an outer ring of large, yellow "ray" florets that attract pollinators, and a central disk of many smaller, fertile "disk" florets that mature into seeds. Leaves: They have large, oval, toothed leaves that are rough to the touch. Heliotropism: Immature flower buds exhibit heliotropism, moving to follow the sun from east to west. Once mature, the flower heads typically face east
